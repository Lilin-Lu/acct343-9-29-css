General Ledger & Key Accounts

Welcome to a simplified overview of basic accounting principles. This page illustrates the fundamental elements of the accounting equation and common account types.

The Accounting Equation

The foundation of double-entry bookkeeping is the **Accounting Equation**, which must always remain in balance:

**Assets = Liabilities + Equity**

Main Account Categories

All accounts fall into one of these five main categories:

  • **Assets:** Resources owned by the company (e.g., Cash, Accounts Receivable).
  • **Liabilities:** Obligations owed to outside parties (e.g., Accounts Payable, Notes Payable).
  • **Equity:** The owners' claim on the assets (e.g., Common Stock, Retained Earnings).
  • **Revenue:** Increases in assets or decreases in liabilities resulting from the delivery of goods or services (e.g., Sales Revenue, Service Revenue).
  • **Expenses:** Decreases in assets or increases in liabilities from the costs of generating revenue (e.g., Rent Expense, Salaries Expense).

Impact on Accounts (Debits & Credits)

Every transaction affects at least two accounts. **Debits** (Dr) and **Credits** (Cr) are used to record these changes.

Normal Balances and Increase/Decrease Rules
Account Category To Increase (Normal Balance) To Decrease
Assets Debit (Dr) Credit (Cr)
Liabilities Credit (Cr) Debit (Dr)
Equity Credit (Cr) Debit (Dr)
Revenue Credit (Cr) Debit (Dr)
Expenses Debit (Dr) Credit (Cr)

Understanding these rules is essential for preparing the **Trial Balance** and ultimately, the **Financial Statements**.

Basic Financial Statements

  1. Income Statement (shows Revenue and Expenses)
  2. Statement of Retained Earnings (shows changes in Equity)
  3. Balance Sheet (shows Assets, Liabilities, and Equity)
  4. Statement of Cash Flows (shows cash movements)
